📖 Executive Summary

The Suns arrive in Oklahoma City facing a 0-1 hole after Sunday's 119-84 defeat. The diagnostics are clear: 17 turnovers that OKC converted into 34 points, 34.9% shooting, and a hustle identity that didn't show up. Game 2 is about whether the corrections hold — not about re-litigating Sunday.

Vegas is not hedging. The line has widened from OKC -14 in Game 1 to OKC -17.5 tonight — the largest Game 2 spread of these playoffs. The moneyline implies a ~95% probability of another Thunder win. Oddsmakers are pricing in a sweep.

But availability is a live story. Grayson Allen went through drills Tuesday and appears close to a return after missing four games — his spacing would fundamentally change a Suns offense that collapsed into isolation in Game 1. Jordan Goodwin (calf, re-aggravated in G1 Q2) and Mark Williams (foot stress reaction, still in a walking boot) are also questionable. If any combination returns, the rotation finally breathes. If not, the minutes burden on Booker, Brooks, and Green compounds against the NBA's best defense.

Ott's public directive for Game 2 is structural: fewer turnovers, faster pace, more ball movement, less isolation. Execution — not effort — decides whether this stays a series. Grayson Allen trending toward return would give the rotation a real floor-spacer and ease the ball-pressure load on Booker.
