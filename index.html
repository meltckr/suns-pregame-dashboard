📖 Executive Summary

The Phoenix Suns have arrived. After fighting through two play-in games — including a dominant 111-96 win over Golden State on Friday where Jalen Green erupted for 36 points on 14-of-20 shooting — Phoenix has earned the right to face the defending champion Oklahoma City Thunder. The Suns are alive, in rhythm, and carrying serious momentum into Paycom Center.

The challenge is immense. OKC posted the NBA's best record (64-18), best defense (107.7 DRTG, #1), and best net rating (+11.1). Shai Gilgeous-Alexander (31.1 PPG, 55.3% FG) has averaged 30 points on 50.9% shooting in five meetings against Phoenix this season. The Thunder are 14-point favorites and -3000 in the series — the largest first-round spread of these playoffs. Every expert is picking OKC in 4 or 5.

But there are cracks to exploit. OKC's 3-point defense ranks just 25th in the NBA, and the Suns set a franchise record with 1,210 threes this season. Jalen Williams has been limited to 33 games and his shooting has dipped sharply. OKC has had a full 7 days of rest since April 12 — rust is a real factor. And Jordan Goodwin's play-in performance (19 pts, 6 steals) showed Phoenix has the defensive identity to compete. The Suns enter as massive underdogs, but they enter with momentum and nothing to lose.

The X-factor: Green did not play in any of the 5 regular-season games vs OKC. The Thunder have zero film on what he looks like at full speed. After 36 points against Golden State, he arrives as the most dangerous unknown in this series. That's a genuine edge. The Suns don't need to win the series today — they need to show OKC this will not be a walkover.
