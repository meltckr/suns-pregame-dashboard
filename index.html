📖 Executive Summary

This is it. One game. No margin. The Phoenix Suns host the Golden State Warriors in the NBA Play-In Tournament elimination game — winner earns the 8th seed and a first-round date with the Oklahoma City Thunder, the top seed in the West. Loser cleans out their locker and goes home. After blowing a 7v8 shot on Tuesday, tonight is the only path left.

Tuesday's collapse against Portland still stings. The Suns led by 11 in the fourth quarter before Deni Avdija took over — posting 41 points, capping it with a go-ahead 3-point play with just 16.1 seconds on the clock. Devin Booker's 5-foul night limited his aggressiveness, and Jalen Green's potential tying 3 rimmed out at the buzzer. Tonight is the redemption game. The bounce-back or the burial.

Golden State arrives riding an emotional high. Steph Curry dropped 35 points in 36 minutes Wednesday against LAC, orchestrating a stunning 13-point fourth-quarter comeback. It was quintessential Curry playoff mode — but note he may be operating under a minute restriction after some ankle management. Kristaps Porzingis (20/5/5 in that game) is now questionable with ankle soreness from that effort. His status is the single biggest injury wildcard tonight.

The math favors Phoenix: 25-16 at Mortgage Matchup Center this season, -3.5 favorites, 64.8% win probability. But GSW owns the season series 3-1, and the Suns' only win was a nail-biting 99-98 at home on December 18. Curry has made a career of erasing conventional wisdom in May. Home crowd, redemption fuel, and Jordan Ott's adjustments give Phoenix the edge — but this is not a comfortable spot. Every possession tonight is a playoff possession.
